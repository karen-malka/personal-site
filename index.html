My Design Process

I first identify what exactly I am creating and why: who is the audience? what are they looking for?

Then I research and explore what my client(s)/user(s) goals are: including noting past attempts at solutions, conducting user interviews, discovering the current market

Planning and Design are key aspects of the process where I create wireframes, sketches, and outlines with an emphasis on finding a balance between common design patterns and new creative aspects

Once I prepared the user interface, I enhance the user experience with prototyping where I build the mockup of the product, highlighting usability as top priority

The final step is testing where I ensure that my design is fit for the user(s)/client(s), and I analyze the results and repeat the process, if necessary!

Waterford Research Website

This website was made for the Research team at Waterford.org. Prior to this, the nonprofit organization had a general website encompassing all parts of their purpose, though lacked focus on the Research portion of their organization. Thus, this website was set into motion!

The client had the following goals:

  • promote organization's software by showcasing its content in engaging ways (modern and visually appealing)
  • emphasize strong focus on research (improving efficacy and stands out against competition)
  • encourage people to provide funding for organization to meet its program goals
  • make research more presentable and relevant with PDF previews and summaries

Art Alcove Website

This website was made to provide users with a way to discover different types of art materials that would most appropriately fit their desired art project.

Users type a query into the search bar; for example:

  • I want to paint a landscape using blue and green watercolors, cheap
  • I need high quality colored pencils and markers
  • I want to create a drawing with fountain pens but I only have $5

The IR system pulls the the art material data from Blick, an art site, and it determines its recommendations based on word matching with the query (using a technique called cosine similarity), a text mining aspect (specifically SVD) for interpretability, and product reviews.
