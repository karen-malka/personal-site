Waterford Research Website

Overview

This website was made for the Research team at Waterford.org. Prior to this, the nonprofit organization had a general website encompassing all parts of their purpose. However, there was not much focus on the Research portion of their organization. Thus, this website was set into motion!

The client had the following goals:

  • create a modern, visually appealing site
  • promote organization's software by showcasing its content in engaging ways
  • emphasize strong focus on research (imrpoving efficacy and stands out against competition)
  • encourage people to provide funding for organization to meet its program goals
  • make research more presentable and relevant with PDF previews and summaries

I conducted user interviews to answer the following question: How can Waterford present their research on a website effectively for users? or in other words, what are their goals?

The results revealed the following user goals:

  • understand the emphasis on research and its effectiveness
  • comprehend the research process
  • easily find research evidence through large focus on visual data

Reflect and Revise

Usability testing revealed an issue with the quotations on the Family Engagement page.

Initially, on the Family Engagement page, there was a sidebar of quotations. However, it seemed too cluttered and was not very usable. Thus, I created a carousel of quotes to put at the bottom of the page.
